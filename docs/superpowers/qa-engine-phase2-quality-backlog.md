## ŌÜĀ’ĖÅ Pull-forward ŌĆö two HIGH items the audit recommends NOT waiting for full Phase 2

These remain behaviorally Phase-2, but a *behavior-neutral slice* should land before cutover because
the failure mode is silent, not a crash:

- **CRL-02 ŌĆö `LearningStore.selectRules()` has no `app` parameter (HIGH).** Nothing in the port, the
  store interface, or the constructor documents that the Plan-6 wiring closure MUST capture `app`.
  Deferring relies on a future human remembering an undocumented invariant; the failure mode at
  cutover is **silent cross-app rule contamination** (every app's learned rules returned to every
  run) ŌĆö exactly the Goodhart corruption this system exists to prevent. **Pull the typed contract
  forward now**: add `app: string` to the port signature as a compile-time obligation (the v1 stub
  ignores it). Behavior-neutral (the stub never reads it), but the cutover wiring then physically
  cannot compile without supplying `app`. Converts a tribal-knowledge landmine into a compiler error.

- **OS-04 ŌĆö V8 source-map (bundled-deploy) coverage path completely unpinned (HIGH).** The VLQ
  decoder that is the keystone for bundled-app (Angular/React) change-coverage has zero parity
  coverage. Defer reasoning is honest (a wrong fixture is worse than none), but "the keystone of our
  only anti-Goodhart signal is untested" is not a comfortable HIGH to leave. **Pull forward the
  lift-the-existing-ORACLE-03-fixture slice** as a named pre-cutover hardening task.
